I am interested in faces. Through the opportunity to study social robotics, I was intrigued by how much humans pay attention to the human face. For this project, I wanted to explore how information overlay occurs on this most social of interfaces.
This is a small exploration of a particular information overlay on the face, the bindi.
The cultural significance of the bindi is very complex, with a rich history, socio-political context, and place in contemporary popular culture.
Without attempting to capture all its complexity, without attempting to summarize the diversity of those that choose to wear the bindi or classify it as one thing, what I find most interesting is how information can be derived from such a simple design because of where it is placed.
